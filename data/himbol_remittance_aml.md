# Himbol Policy: Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Remittance Controls

**Document Reference: POL-COMP-AML04**
**Effective Date: January 1, 2026**

## 1. Suspicious Activity Monitoring and Red Flags

As a leading remittance provider, Himbol enforces rigid Anti-Money Laundering (AML) monitoring protocols. Tellers must flag any individual receiving inbound international remittances from more than three separate global senders within a single rolling 7-day window. Splitting large remittance payouts into smaller chunks across consecutive days to deliberately bypass reporting thresholds (structuring) is explicitly illegal and must be instantly reported to the Compliance Director at the Bahty Meskerem Square HQ.

## 2. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Triggers

Any inbound or outbound remittance transaction equaling or exceeding 100,000 ERN automatically triggers Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD). The processing agent must complete an EDD Form 4A, capturing verifiable details regarding the sender’s relationship to the recipient, the verified source of funds, and the precise economic purpose of the transfer. Transaction holds will remain active for up to 48 hours until formal written clearance is issued by the legal compliance department.
