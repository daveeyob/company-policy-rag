# Himbol Policy: International Airport Currency Exchange Rules

**Document Reference: POL-FX-AP02**
**Effective Date: January 1, 2026**

## 1. Mandatory Exchange Procedures and Scope

The Himbol operational branches located exclusively inside the Asmara International Airport are designated strictly as Currency Exchange points. These specialized booths are legally barred from initiating standard bank account creation, processing cash deposits into remote accounts, or resolving M-Nakfa wallet verification discrepancies. All incoming international travelers must be provided with an official, serially numbered Himbol Foreign Exchange Receipt upon completing transactions.

## 2. Exchange Limits and Identity Controls

Airport exchange desks must log the passport number, flight details, and visa information for every individual transaction exceeding an equivalent value of $500 USD. Staff must verify physical cash banknotes for authenticity using the ultraviolet scanners provided at each teller terminal before issuing local currency. No single teller may maintain a cash float exceeding $10,000 USD or equivalent in foreign currencies at the workstation terminal; all excess funds must be dropped securely into the dual-custody under-counter time-locked safe every 4 hours.
